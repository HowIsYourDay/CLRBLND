Right off the bat it's important to recognize that digital media faces very similar problems as non-digital media, but has different restrictions as to how it can address accessibility issues. This is to say that digital media has many abilities to help create a more accessible web. The W3C (World Wide Web Consortium) put in place WAI-WCAG (Web Accessibility Initiative - Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) standards. These provide great resources and rulesets for accessibility on the web. Despite this, it is quite common to come across a lack of accessibility built into websites, or even worse, accessibility built in incorrectly. This is detrimental to users of the web, the web which is fundamentally designed to work for everyone.

Since the web is just a vessel to viewing digital media and graphic design, we can split these two topics. Consider that a piece of digital content, such as a graphical element, could either be accessible to view, or not. In the case that this content is inaccessible, the web acts as a filter that can correct these issues, or not.

It isn't necessarily problematic that a certain piece of content is inaccessible by default. Think about a painting in the context of someone who has a visual disability that limits or completely restricts vision. It is almost impossible to design a painting alone to be accessible in the case that someone cannot see, to no fault of the creator. However, we can add in context like alternative texts that describes what is pictured. Of course, this is necessary for accessibility, but isn't sufficient.

Accessibility issues like colorblindness may construct a false image, which is detrimental in its own way. Vision deficiencies like colorblindness are not the only accessibility issue that people might face though. Furthermore, inaccessible design can cause issues for everyone, even those who don't face accessibility issues.

Websites with clear navigation, logical layouts, and responsive UIs; Digital designs with thoughtful color choices, simple patterns, and readable text all help to create accessible experiences that are enjoyable for all users.

Modern digital design stands at the intersection of creativity, technology, and responsibility. We need to consider accessibility as a necessity by creating responsive and inclusive pieces. We need to innovate and inspire accessibility in other designs. I believe we can make progress in accessibility, for a brighter more inclusive future.
